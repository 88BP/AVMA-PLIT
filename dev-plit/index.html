our brand story
In 2014, AVMA PLIT embarked on a comprehensive research study to understand the insurance needs of veterinarians. We interviewed over 1,500 current, former and potential AVMA PLIT participants and listened to their thoughts on our insurance program. Everything we learned from them culminated in the new AVMA PLIT: Protecting you through it all initiative you see here today. And it’s more than simply a new look. This exciting new initiative will ultimately enhance AVMA members’ understanding of the insurance solutions and resources available to them through comprehensive tools and educational programs that help veterinarians minimize their risk of facing claims and help them better understand their business needs.

“For more than 50 years we’ve helped protect the reputations of veterinarians at all stages of their careers and helped guide them through tough legal situations and license complaints. AVMA members continue to tell us that the PLIT program is one of the most valuable components of their AVMA membership; we want to build on our success and ensure that our colleagues are aware of the full scope of products and services—from professional liability to business and personal insurance.”

Janet Donlin, DVM, AVMA PLIT CEO

research findings
The opinions of the veterinarian community were instrumental in guiding us on how we can serve AVMA members even better. What we heard again and again from the veterinarians we spoke with was they deemed it absolutely essential that they participate in a program that is overseen by veterinarians, tailored to the needs of veterinarians and veterinary practices.
Other key findings include:
  • The AVMA PLIT insurance is viewed as a top benefit of AVMA membership. More than 60,000 AVMA members are PLIT policyholders.
  • About one in four respondents admit they do not fully understand their malpractice policy.
  • Nearly all respondents are aware that the PLIT gives them access to malpractice and license defense coverage, more than one-third (34%) are not certain that PLIT provided business property and general liability insurance, and homeowners insurance (26%).
  • 80 percent of respondents indicate that it is essential that veterinarians oversee their insurance program. Almost a third of respondents are not aware that the PLIT is managed by veterinarians.
our brand positioning
After we completed our research study, we delved into creating our strategic platform to better align our mission and effectively communicate that to all types of veterinarians. Once we distilled everything we learned from the veterinarian community, we crafted our new brand positioning. This message will serve as the foundation for communicating everything we do for the veterinarian community through our marketing and our daily operations:
The AVMA PLIT is your trusted professional insurance partner, delivering the most complete and supportive program available to veterinarians, so you can always be confident you have everything you need to remain protected.
research findings
After establishing our brand positioning, we partnered with a branding agency to create our refreshed look. Over a few months, they created a range of engaging and visually diverse concepts that brought the brand positioning to life. We then presented those concepts to a focus group of veterinarians to learn which concepts were generating the most positive reaction. Their feedback ultimately shaped the finalized look and tone of our refreshed brand, including everything from the tagline to the logo choice to the color palette. Other key findings include:
  • Overall, veterinarians preferred images that showed veterinarians interacting with animals because they convey real and relatable situations.
  • The new logo rose to the top because AVMA and PLIT are of equal size, reflecting an equal partnership, with the bold treatment promoting PLIT while demonstrating that AVMA is an integral part of the package.
  • Many preferred the “Protecting you through it all” tagline because it suggests they can call PLIT for help and work with veterinarians to solve problems.
  • The phrases and headlines were felt to be very meaningful and believable and did the best job of conveying AVMA PLIT’s expertise and specialization.
  • The color palette elicited a more relaxed and comfortable response, while making AVMA PLIT feel more approachable and making it easier to digest insurance information.
